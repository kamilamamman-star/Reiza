The Story

About
REIZA

REIZA was built from the inside of Nigeria's oil and gas industry — not from a boardroom, and not from a distance. It was built by an Engineer who has spent years in the operational heart of upstream Nigeria: reporting, analysing NPT, coordinating contractors, and sitting in the post-well debriefs where the real lessons live.

That experience is the foundation of everything REIZA does. When we advise on drilling costs, we're drawing on direct knowledge of what those costs look like from inside the rig floor. When we write about geothermal potential in West Africa, we're drawing on the same engineering principles applied every day in petroleum wells.

REIZA exists because Nigeria's energy sector — and Africa's energy transition broadly — needs more indigenous voices, more indigenous expertise, and more indigenous ownership. Not just participation. Ownership.

The consultancy work we do today funds the vision we're building for tomorrow: a premium EV charging network, designed and owned by an indigenous Nigerian energy professional, as a statement that the transition belongs to us too.
